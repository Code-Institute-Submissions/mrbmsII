Greens In Regulation (GIR)

What is it?

Measures the % of times a player gets on the green in regulation. Regulation is 2 shots less than par. For example, on a par 4, regulation would be getting on the green in 2 shots.

GIR 200+ Yards

What is it?

Measures the % of times a player gets on the green in regulation when the shot to get on in regulation is over 200 yards.

Scrambling (Up and Down)

What is it?

Measures the % of times a player achieves par when they don't get on the green in regulation. For example, if on a par 4 a player took 3 shots to get on the green yet they "scrambled" and still made par.

Sand Saves

What is it?

Measures the % of times a player achieves par when one of their shots before went into a bunker.

How it Works

These are various PGA Tour statistics from the years 2002 - 2019. Each statistic has various data points associated with it. These data points can been visualised graphically via a line chart or in a numerical list format.

Selecting a year from the dropdown control will dynamically update the data points for each statistic, thereby automatically refreshing the line charts as well as the numerical data list sections.

Clicking the "View Chart" button will expand a section that shows the selected statistic and its data points in a line chart, whilst clicking the "By The Numbers" button will show data for the selected statistic in a list format.
